# 🤝 Code of Conduct — RescueBOT Community

**Project:** RescueBOT — Autonomous Disaster Response & Rescue Robot
**Team:** BOT THINGS | ZYRO 2026 | Kalyani Government Engineering College

---

## Our Pledge

We, the maintainers and contributors of **RescueBOT**, pledge to make participation in this project and our community a **harassment-free, inclusive, and welcoming experience** for everyone — regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are committed to building an open, respectful, and collaborative community around a project that aims to save lives. That mission demands the highest standard of conduct from everyone involved.

---

## Our Standards

### ✅ Behavior We Encourage

- **Respectful communication** — Use professional, constructive, and thoughtful language in all interactions.
- **Technical collaboration** — Share knowledge, review code with care, and give feedback that improves the project.
- **Empathy and patience** — Recognize that contributors have varying skill levels. Beginners are welcome.
- **Constructive criticism** — Disagree with ideas, not people. Focus on the technical merit of proposals.
- **Acknowledging contributions** — Credit others' work appropriately in PRs, issues, and documentation.
- **Transparency** — Document decisions, experiments, and design choices openly.
- **Safety consciousness** — This project involves physical hardware and emergency response systems. Report potential safety issues immediately.

### ❌ Behavior That Will Not Be Tolerated

- Harassment, intimidation, or threatening language of any kind.
- Discriminatory jokes, comments, or references related to any protected characteristic.
- Publishing others' private information (real name, address, contact details) without explicit permission — **doxxing**.
- Deliberate sabotage of the codebase, firmware, or documentation.
- Posting misleading technical information that could compromise hardware safety.
- Sustained disruptive behavior in discussions, issues, or pull requests.
- Sexual or violent content in any community space (Issues, Discussions, PRs, comments).
- Personal attacks or insults directed at contributors or maintainers.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All content in this GitHub repository (code, issues, pull requests, discussions, wiki).
- External spaces where you represent RescueBOT (social media, hackathons, conferences, demos).
- Direct messages to other contributors via GitHub or any other platform when discussing project-related topics.

---

## Reporting a Violation

If you witness or experience behavior that violates this Code of Conduct:

1. **Directly contact the project maintainer:**
   - **Prolayjit Biswas** — [@Prolayjit-B14](https://github.com/Prolayjit-B14)
   - Via GitHub DM or the email listed on the GitHub profile.

2. **All reports are confidential.** Your identity will not be disclosed to the reported party.

3. **Include in your report:**
   - A description of the incident.
   - Links to relevant issues, comments, or messages (if applicable).
   - Any context that may help us understand the situation.

---

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for enforcing this Code of Conduct. Upon receiving a report, we will:

1. **Review** the incident within 72 hours.
2. **Respond** to the reporter with the outcome.
3. Take one of the following actions based on severity:

| Severity | Action |
|:---|:---|
| Minor / First offense | Private warning with explanation |
| Repeated / Moderate | Temporary ban from contributing (Issues/PRs/Discussions) |
| Severe / Malicious | Permanent ban and repository block |

Maintainers who do not enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may face consequences as determined by the project's core team.
